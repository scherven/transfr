What the map earns its space with

━
Shape recognition. Two options can take wildly different paths through the country — you feel that instantly on a map, never from a text list.
◉
Risk, placed. The one tight change isn't a word in a row — it's a coloured dot you can point at, on the city where it happens.
◈
Grounding. Faint neighbours (Berlin, Köln, München…), the coastline and the borders you cross tell you where you are without reading a single label.

How it behaves

✓
Opt-in, gracefully. First plan asks for location. Decline and “From” simply falls back to an empty field / last station — no dead ends.
✓
Nearest station, not raw GPS. We snap your coordinate to the nearest reachable station for routing, and keep the exact point for the live “you” dot.
✓
Still just a field. Type, paste, swap, pick a recent — current-location is one more way in, never the only one.
•
Uses CLLocationManager, while-in-use authorization. No background tracking in this scope.
04

Two ways to draw the map

This is the real fork, and the one I'd like your call on. Both draw the exact same route overlay — the only question is what sits underneath it.

Paper map Recommended

Vector, drawn by us — the maps you see all over this page.
  • ✓ Matches the app exactly, light & dark, offline.
  • ✓ Free; no keys, no tile bill, no attribution bar.
  • ✓ Perfect for thumbnails — 30 tiny maps cost nothing.
  • ✗ Not zoomable to street level; it's a schematic, not a survey.

Apple Maps

Native MapKit tiles under the same overlay (mocked here — real tiles can't load in this preview).
  • ✓ A true map: pan, zoom, streets, real geography.
  • ✓ Free on iOS, no API key, first-party.
  • ✗ Heavier; its look is Apple's, not ours.
  • ✗ Wants network; snapshots per card get expensive.

My recommendation: ship the paper map everywhere first — it's consistent, cheap, and offline, which suits a transit app. If you want a true zoomable map on the single full-screen connection view, we add MapKit there only and keep the vector thumbnails on cards. Best of both, one screen at a time.
